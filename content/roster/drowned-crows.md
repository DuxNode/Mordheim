*"The Crow keeps a ledger, and we are all in it."*

*They sailed south under Onogal's eye to collect what was owed Him — a tithe of plunder and plague-dead, promised and long unpaid. Through a broken oath they failed to take it, and the Crow does not forgive a debt; He collects it. The storm He sent did not blow them off course so much as aim them: south past every friendly shore, down the Black Gulf, until the longship Drowned Crow broke her back on the rocks near Aldium and the sea took her whole. Those He still had use for crawled from the surf coughing brine and feathers, the rot already blooming beneath their skin — drowned men not permitted to return home until the ledger is paid in full, in plunder and in blood.*
