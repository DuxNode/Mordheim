Magic in Mordheim is divided into distinct spell lists, each tied to specific warband types or hero roles. A wizard starts with one randomly determined spell from his list and may learn more through experience. To cast a spell, roll 2D6 and meet or beat the spell's Difficulty. A wizard may cast one spell per turn and cannot wear armour or carry a shield or buckler (the sole exception being the Prayers of Sigmar).

The following spell lists are used by warbands in the Mordheim Australis 2027 campaign.

## Spell Lists

**[[reference/spells/lesser-magic|Lesser Magic]]**
Available to Warlocks (hired swords) and to any Hero who has learned magic via a Tome of Magic. The most accessible spell list for human warbands.

**[[reference/spells/chaos-rituals|Chaos Rituals]]**
Used by the Magister of the Cult of the Possessed, Beastmen shamans, and the Marauders of Chaos Seer with the Mark of Chaos Undivided.

**[[reference/spells/necromancy|Necromancy]]**
Used by the Necromancer of the Undead warband. Grants power over the dead — raising fallen warriors, terrorising enemies, and draining the living.

**[[reference/spells/prayers-of-sigmar|Prayers of Sigmar]]**
Used by the Sigmarite Matriarch (Sisters of Sigmar) and the Warrior Priest (Witch Hunters). Unlike other magic, Prayers may be used in armour and are not subject to anti-spell protections.

**[[reference/spells/skaven-magic|Skaven Magic]]**
Formally the Magic of the Horned Rat — used by the Warlock Engineer of the core Skaven warband and by Sorcerers of Clan Eshin and Clan Pestilens.

**[[reference/spells/nurgle-rituals|Nurgle Rituals]]**
Used by the Carnival Master of the Carnival of Chaos. Diseases, rot, and the blessings of the Plague God.

**[[reference/spells/rituals-of-hashut|Rituals of Hashut]]**
Used by the Chaos Dwarf Sorcerer. Dark fire-and-ash sorcery in the name of Hashut, the Father of Darkness.

> [!note] Warband-specific spell lists
> Several other warbands in the campaign use spell lists not covered here — including the Marauders of Chaos Seer variants (Tchar, Onogal, Shornaal Rituals). These are documented on each warband's page.
